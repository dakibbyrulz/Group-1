# PIR Sensor
the PIR sensor, or 'Passive Infrared Sensor', has two pyroelectric sensors, one as the base-line and one that 'senses' if someone is in its range.
there were tests done to determine both the angle of view and range limit of the sensor.

# Testing Plans and Overall Results
For the Field of View, i took a protractor and set up the sensor so that the horizontal would be at the 0/180 line and then slowly moved my finger around the protractor until i got a reading.

The angles were around 17.8degrees, so multiplying that by 2 and subtracting it from 180 gives us our FOV of 144.4degrees.
Comparing this with the data sheet that is provided in the pictures section, we find that the normal range of is less that 120degrees within 7meters.

While it is true that my testing was done on a much smaller scale, 144.4 degrees is still around 20% higher than 120.

For the range of the sensor, i used myself as a heat source and slowly walked towards the sensor while out of its range, to read my screen from far away, i had a pair of binoculars.

the results of this test ended up being around 24ft from the front.
the data sheet does not give an outright range limit, but it does say that the range is 7 meters within 120degrees, as i was in front of the sensor, this number should be accurate.

7 meters is roughly 23ft, which means that my sensor was approx. 4.3% higher range than the documented amount.
